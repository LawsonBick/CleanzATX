Kate Durham

My Story

Built from a decision to eat clean.

I grew up not knowing what was being put into my food. For years I dealt with gut issues I couldn't explain, cycling through every elimination diet a twenty-year-old can find on the internet. Nothing stuck — until I made the decision to eat clean for a full year.

Everything changed. My energy, my health, my relationship with food — all of it shifted. I started noticing what was missing from my plate and what had quietly been added to it. And once I saw it, I couldn't un-see it.

I believe we were meant to eat what God put on this earth — not the man-made chemicals the food industry puts in our meals. Everything I cook is farm to table: fresh product, real food, the way it was intended. That's the Sunday Harvest promise.

Culinary School Graduate Michelin Restaurant Experience 20 Years Old & Building Something Real

Why Clean Food Matters

The difference you feel is the difference that matters.

For a long time, I thought feeling tired after dinner was normal. I thought stomach aches were part of being human. They're not. Real food — the kind that grows on a farm, not the kind that ships in a truck with a 14-month shelf life — doesn't leave you heavy. It leaves you satisfied.

Cooking farm-to-table isn't a trend for me. It's the only way I cook. When I'm in your kitchen, every ingredient is chosen for what it is, not for what it's been processed into. Fresh produce from local growers when the season allows, clean proteins, whole fats, real seasoning. Nothing from a bag that promises "flavor" in parentheses.

My philosophy extends to every dietary preference. Gluten-free, dairy-free, vegan, paleo, allergies to nuts or shellfish — I see every restriction as a chance to learn something new. It's rare I hear a request I haven't already planned around.

We were meant to eat what God put on this earth. That's the Sunday Harvest promise.
