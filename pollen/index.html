Why Lakeway Windows Get So Yellow

Mountain cedar pollen season hits the Lake Travis corridor every winter and spring — and it's one of the worst in the country. The fine yellow dust penetrates screens, bonds to glass with morning dew, and builds up on every horizontal surface.

By the time pollen season peaks, most Lakeway and Bee Cave homeowners are dealing with windows so coated they look frosted — and no amount of rain washes it off completely.

🌬️
Pollen bonds to glass with moisture Morning dew activates tree pollen's sticky proteins, causing it to adhere to window glass — regular rain doesn't remove it.
🪟
Screens trap it, making it worse Pollen particles get caught in screen mesh, turning screens into yellow filters that block light and spread dust inside when opened.
💧
Hard water multiplies the problem Lakeway's limestone-heavy water leaves mineral deposits when pollen-contaminated water dries on glass — creating a permanent haze without professional treatment.
