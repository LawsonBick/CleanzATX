Why It Happens

Pollen Season Is Brutal on Lake Travis Windows

Mountain cedar in Central Texas produces one of the highest pollen counts in the country — and it leaves a mess that rain can't fix.

🌿
Pollen Bonds to Glass

Morning dew activates sticky proteins in cedar pollen, causing it to fuse to window glass. Rain water can't break that bond — it just smears it around.

🪟
Screens Trap It Inside

Window screens catch pollen particles in their mesh, turning into yellow filters. Every time you open a window, pollen pours in and allergy season gets worse.

💧
Hard Water Makes It Permanent

Lakeway's limestone-heavy water leaves mineral deposits when pollen-contaminated water dries on glass — creating a haze that gets harder to remove the longer you wait.
